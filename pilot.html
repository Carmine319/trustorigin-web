Institutional Pilot Programme

The TrustOrigin Institutional Pilot Programme is a structured 30-day engagement designed to evaluate registry integration, ethics transparency articulation, and governance continuity architecture within a controlled institutional environment.

The pilot does not constitute certification, regulatory validation, or formal compliance endorsement. It provides a structured documentation evaluation period enabling institutions to assess integration feasibility and governance clarity enhancement.

The objective is controlled adoption testing rather than theoretical demonstration.

Pilot Objectives

The pilot is structured around the following objectives:

  • Evaluate declarative registry integration within existing governance workflows
  • Assess Ethics Transparency articulation suitability for board-level reporting
  • Map vendor dependency posture within structured documentation framework
  • Test continuity export capability and version freeze protocols
  • Identify internal documentation fragmentation reduction potential

Each objective is assessed through structured drafting sessions and controlled declaration recording.

Pilot Scope

Registry Declarations

Up to 50 structured registry entries covering AI usage declarations, digital artefact registrations, and vendor dependency statements.

Ethics Transparency Drafting

Development of a formal Ethics Transparency Declaration reflecting AI governance posture, oversight models, risk boundaries, and vendor exposure articulation.

Governance Consolidation

Integration of registry declarations and ethics articulation into a structured Governance & Continuity Dossier draft suitable for executive review.

Continuity Export Simulation

Demonstration of deterministic export bundle reflecting declared institutional posture at pilot completion.

Pilot Deliverables

  • Structured Registry Trust Receipts
  • Formal Ethics Transparency Declaration draft
  • Governance & Continuity Dossier outline
  • Migration readiness posture articulation
  • Board-level executive summary document

All deliverables remain declarative in nature and subject to the non-claims boundary of the infrastructure.

Engagement Parameters

The pilot is conducted over a 30-day period with defined drafting sessions and documentation checkpoints. Institutional participants designate an internal governance lead to coordinate structured declaration inputs.

Engagement fees reflect organisational complexity and number of departments involved.

Pilot Engagement Range: £5,000 – £8,500

Post-Pilot Pathway

Following pilot completion, institutions may elect to transition into structured licensing tiers covering Registry-only access, Ethics Transparency integration, Dossier consolidation, or Enterprise Continuity Integration.

Pilot participation does not create ongoing obligation. Institutional adoption decisions remain discretionary.

Non-Claims Boundary

TrustOrigin does not validate legal compliance, certify regulatory conformity, or adjudicate disputes during or after the pilot programme. Responsibility for declaration accuracy remains solely with the participating institution.

The pilot evaluates documentation architecture, not regulatory standing.
