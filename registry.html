Public Declarative Registry

The Public Declarative Registry provides a structured mechanism for recording institutional and individual declarations relating to artificial intelligence usage, digital artefacts, vendor dependency exposure, and technical configuration posture.

Each declaration submitted through the registry is preserved as a time-anchored record supported by cryptographic hashing discipline. The system generates a Trust Receipt reflecting the existence of the declared content at the moment of submission.

The registry does not validate accuracy. It preserves declared existence.

Why a Declarative Registry Is Necessary

AI-assisted outputs are increasingly integrated into research papers, financial analysis, healthcare documentation, procurement assessments, and operational decision-support environments. At the same time, vendor reliance on model providers, cloud platforms, and API integrations introduces layered technical exposure.

Without structured documentation, institutional memory becomes fragmented. Statements about system usage are often informal. Vendor exposure mapping may not be preserved longitudinally. Version evolution of artefacts may not be clearly recorded.

The registry introduces disciplined documentation continuity in environments where retrospective reconstruction can otherwise become unreliable.

Declaration Categories

AI Usage Declaration

Formal articulation of AI deployment scope, model reference, operational context, and declared oversight structure. Suitable for research documentation, internal governance records, and procurement transparency contexts.

Digital Artefact Registration

Time-anchored registration of documents, datasets, reports, model outputs, configuration files, and governance statements. Each registration produces a Trust Receipt reflecting hash integrity at declaration time.

Vendor Dependency Disclosure

Structured declaration of infrastructure reliance, including model providers, cloud hosting platforms, API integrations, and external service dependencies influencing operational output.

Version Lineage Mapping

Declaration of artefact evolution over time, enabling preservation of version relationships without altering original documentation state.

Structural Characteristics

  • Time-anchored declaration preservation
  • Cryptographic hashing discipline
  • Non-adjudicative documentation framework
  • Version-aware continuity mapping
  • Deterministic Trust Receipt generation

The registry does not evaluate content validity, intellectual property ownership, regulatory compliance, or ethical conformity. It records declared statements without assuming certification authority.

Institutional Application

The registry layer may be adopted independently by research teams, start-up organisations, or enterprise departments requiring structured documentation of AI usage or artefact evolution.

For broader institutional integration, the registry layer forms the foundational component of the Ethics Transparency Framework and the Governance & Continuity Dossier.
