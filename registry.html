Public Declarative Registry

The Public Declarative Registry enables individuals and organisations to formally record structured declarations relating to artificial intelligence usage, digital artefacts, system configurations, vendor dependencies, and technical posture statements.

Each registry submission produces a time-anchored Trust Receipt reflecting cryptographic hashing of declared content at the moment of submission. The registry preserves declared state without introducing validation authority.

Purpose of the Registry

In contemporary technical environments, documentation often lacks durable anchoring. Artefacts evolve, AI models are updated, vendors change infrastructure, and internal configurations shift over time. When disputes arise or regulatory scrutiny emerges, retrospective reconstruction of declared posture can become difficult.

The registry provides a structured mechanism for preserving declared position at a defined point in time.

It does not verify correctness. It records declared existence.

Registry Declaration Types

AI Usage Declaration

Structured articulation of AI system usage scope, declared purpose, oversight model, and version reference. Suitable for research outputs, internal workflows, or public reporting contexts.

Digital Artefact Registration

Registration of documents, reports, model outputs, datasets, or configuration files, preserving a hash-anchored record of declared existence without asserting ownership validation.

Vendor Dependency Statement

Formal declaration of third-party infrastructure reliance, API integrations, cloud hosting dependencies, or model provider exposure at a defined point in time.

Structural Characteristics

  • Cryptographic hashing discipline
  • Time-anchored declaration recording
  • Version lineage preservation
  • Non-adjudicative architecture
  • Deterministic documentation output

TrustOrigin does not certify intellectual property ownership, regulatory compliance, ethical conformity, or legal validity of submitted declarations. Responsibility remains with the declarant party.
