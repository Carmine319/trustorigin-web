Public Declarative Registry

The TrustOrigin Registry provides structured recording of digital artefacts, AI system usage declarations, vendor dependency statements, and continuity posture records.

All entries generate time-anchored, cryptographically hashed records. TrustOrigin does not certify, validate, verify, or adjudicate submitted declarations.

Two Structured Entry Paths

Individual & Developer Entry

For creators, developers, researchers, and independent operators seeking neutral documentation of digital artefact existence or AI usage declarations.

  • Digital artefact declaration
  • AI usage statement
  • Version lineage mapping
  • Trust Receipt issuance
Register Declaration

Institutional Structured Entry

For organisations requiring structured governance recording across AI systems, vendor dependency exposure, migration readiness posture, and operational continuity.

  • Organisational AI system declaration
  • Vendor dependency documentation
  • Continuity posture statements
  • Deterministic export bundles
Institutional Framework

Registry Output

Each declaration produces a Trust Receipt containing:

  • Declared identifier
  • Cryptographic hash at submission
  • UTC timestamp
  • Declared scope and contextual metadata
  • Version relationship mapping (where applicable)

A Trust Receipt reflects recorded declaration data. It does not represent certification, ownership confirmation, legal status, compliance validation, or authenticity verification.

Operational Boundary

TrustOrigin records structured statements. It does not:

  • Verify submitted artefacts
  • Assess regulatory compliance
  • Determine legality
  • Provide certification authority
  • Resolve disputes
TrustOrigin is a neutral declarative governance infrastructure.
