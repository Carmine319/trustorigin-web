Governance Charter

This Charter defines the structural boundaries, operational scope, and declarative architecture of the TrustOrigin registry.

1. Non-Claims Architecture

  • TrustOrigin does not certify compliance.
  • TrustOrigin does not provide legal advice.
  • TrustOrigin does not validate factual accuracy.
  • TrustOrigin does not audit systems.
  • TrustOrigin does not adjudicate disputes.

All registry entries reflect declarant submissions at the time of recording.

2. Registry Discipline

  • Cryptographic hash generation at declaration time
  • UTC timestamp anchoring
  • Structured metadata schema
  • Version relationship mapping
  • Deterministic export formatting

Registry outputs are descriptive records. They are not certificates.

3. Version & Continuity Control

  • Declared version identifiers
  • Parent-child lineage capability
  • Structured freeze-state documentation
  • Export bundle determinism

TrustOrigin preserves record state at defined moments. It does not modify operational systems.

4. Institutional Engagement Boundary

Institutional Governance Programmes result in structured declarative documentation. Outputs do not constitute regulatory approval, compliance confirmation, or legal certification.

5. Governance Revision Policy

This Charter is version-controlled. Amendments are documented and dated. Historical versions remain referenceable.

TrustOrigin is a neutral declarative governance infrastructure.
