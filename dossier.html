Institutional Governance & Continuity Dossier

The Institutional Governance & Continuity Dossier is a structured documentation framework designed to consolidate registry declarations, ethics transparency articulation, vendor dependency mapping, and migration readiness posture into a coherent institutional record.

Where the Public Registry preserves discrete declarations, the Dossier layer integrates those declarations into a board-level governance structure suitable for regulated environments, research institutions, public bodies, and enterprise-scale organisations.

The Dossier does not certify compliance. It structures declared governance posture in a durable, exportable format.

Purpose of the Dossier Layer

In institutional environments, documentation is often fragmented across departments. AI usage statements may exist separately from vendor risk registers. Ethics articulation may not be formally anchored. Migration readiness considerations may be discussed but not structurally documented.

The Dossier consolidates these dimensions into a unified documentation framework.

It enables institutions to demonstrate documentary continuity, structured governance articulation, and declared risk awareness without asserting regulatory validation.

Dossier Components

Registry Declaration Integration

Consolidation of previously recorded registry declarations including AI usage statements, digital artefact registrations, and vendor dependency disclosures into a structured governance narrative.

Ethics Transparency Articulation

Formal integration of the Ethics Transparency Declaration framework, including oversight models, risk boundaries, and declared AI posture, within the broader institutional governance architecture.

Vendor Dependency Mapping

Structured articulation of cloud infrastructure reliance, API integrations, third-party service exposure, and model provider dependencies at a defined point in time.

Migration Readiness Posture

Declared readiness considerations relating to system migration, vendor substitution feasibility, data portability awareness, and continuity planning frameworks.

Institutional Application Context

The Dossier framework is suitable for institutions operating within regulated or semi-regulated environments where governance clarity, documentation durability, and internal oversight transparency are increasingly expected.

This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Universities and research institutions
  • Financial services organisations
  • Healthcare systems
  • Public sector bodies
  • Technology enterprises deploying AI systems at scale

The framework supports internal governance discussions, board reporting preparation, procurement positioning, and continuity planning articulation.

Export & Continuity Architecture

The Dossier is structured to enable deterministic export bundles reflecting declared posture at defined intervals. Version freeze protocols and annual re-declaration frameworks may be integrated for enterprise clients requiring longitudinal governance documentation.

This design ensures that institutional posture can be preserved in a time-anchored format without dependency on ongoing operational state.

Commercial Structure

The Institutional Dossier is available as a structured engagement layer integrated with Ethics Transparency articulation and Registry declarations. Pricing tiers reflect organisational scale, complexity of vendor exposure, and depth of governance integration required.
