TrustOrigin

TrustOrigin is a neutral recording infrastructure for preserving evidence of existence, declaration, and temporal lineage of digital artefacts.

What TrustOrigin Records

  • Declared digital artefacts
  • Cryptographic hashes
  • Timestamps of declaration
  • Declared scope and context
  • Explicit version and lineage relationships

What TrustOrigin Does Not Do

  • Judge or evaluate content
  • Verify accuracy or truth
  • Certify or endorse artefacts or declarants
  • Determine legality, compliance, or safety
  • Resolve disputes or conflicts

System Output

All TrustOrigin outputs are issued as Trust Receipts. A Trust Receipt is a descriptive record governed by immutable declarations.

A Trust Receipt is not a certificate.

Governance

  • Non-Claims & Boundaries Charter v1.0
  • Trust Receipt Schema v1.0
