Trust Receipts

Trust Receipts are descriptive records of declaration and time. They do not represent certification, correctness, legality, or compliance. Interpretation is the responsibility of the recipient.

What a Trust Receipt Is

A Trust Receipt is a structured record issued by the TrustOrigin system following the declaration of a digital artefact.

It records:

  • The declared artefact identifier
  • The cryptographic hash of the artefact at declaration time
  • The timestamp of declaration
  • The declared scope and context
  • The artefact’s declared temporal lineage

What a Trust Receipt Is Not

  • A certificate
  • A validation of accuracy or truth
  • A statement of legality or compliance
  • An endorsement or approval
  • A judgement of safety, quality, or fitness

Evidentiary Framing

Trust Receipts provide evidence that a declaration occurred at a specific point in time, under a stated scope, using a defined artefact state.

They do not determine how that evidence should be interpreted or relied upon. Any use of a Trust Receipt is contextual and remains the responsibility of the relying party.

TrustOrigin is a neutral recording infrastructure.
