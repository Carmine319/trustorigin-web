TrustOrigin Whitepaper

Version 1.0

1. Executive Summary

TrustOrigin is a neutral declarative governance infrastructure designed to record structured institutional statements relating to AI system usage, digital artefact lineage, vendor dependency exposure, and operational continuity posture.

The system does not certify, validate, adjudicate, or assess artefacts. It records time-anchored, cryptographically hashed declarations in a version-controlled environment.

2. Problem Statement

Modern organisations operate within layered digital infrastructures. AI systems influence outputs. External vendors introduce structural dependency. Artefacts evolve across versions. Migration risk accumulates silently.

Most institutions lack a neutral, timestamped, structured record of what was declared, when, and under which technical configuration.

3. Architectural Principles

  • Neutrality (non-adjudicative design)
  • Time-anchored cryptographic hashing
  • Version lineage preservation
  • Vendor dependency transparency
  • Deterministic export capability
  • Migration readiness articulation

4. Two Structured Layers

Public Declarative Registry

Records artefact declarations and generates Trust Receipts reflecting timestamp and cryptographic hash.

Institutional Governance & Continuity Dossier

Structured documentation addressing vendor exposure, AI usage declarations, migration readiness posture, and deterministic export bundles.

5. Non-Claims Boundary

TrustOrigin does not verify factual accuracy, legal validity, intellectual property ownership, regulatory compliance, or ethical compliance of any submitted artefact or declaration.

The system records declarations. It does not validate them.

6. Institutional Application

TrustOrigin may be used by universities, research institutions, AI development teams, software vendors, and public bodies requiring structured continuity documentation.

7. Conclusion

TrustOrigin provides a structural recording layer for institutional continuity. It does not replace operational systems. It records structured statements about them.
