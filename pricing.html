Institutional Governance & Continuity Programme

TrustOrigin provides structured declarative governance documentation for organisations deploying AI systems, operating within vendor-dependent infrastructures, or seeking continuity documentation across digital environments.

Programme participation results in a structured Institutional Continuity Dossier, registry-anchored declaration records, and deterministic export bundles.

Programme Structure

Foundation Programme

Designed for SMEs and structured teams initiating formal AI governance documentation.

  • AI system declaration object
  • Vendor dependency declaration
  • Continuity posture statement
  • Registry anchoring
  • Structured export bundle

Programme fee typically £2,500 – £3,500

Processing timeframe: 14 days

Structured Programme

Designed for scaling organisations requiring multi-system declaration coverage.

  • Multi-system AI declaration architecture
  • Vendor exposure mapping
  • Migration readiness posture documentation

Programme fee typically £3,500 – £5,500

Institutional Programme

Designed for enterprise and regulated environments requiring expanded continuity documentation.

Engagement Model

All programme engagements begin with a structured institutional intake review. Scope is defined according to organisational architecture, AI system footprint, vendor exposure, and continuity documentation requirements.

TrustOrigin does not provide legal advice, compliance certification, audit opinions, or regulatory endorsement. All outputs are declarative governance records.

Operational Boundary

TrustOrigin records structured declarations relating to governance posture. It does not verify systems, certify compliance, determine legality, or resolve disputes.

TrustOrigin is a neutral declarative governance infrastructure.
